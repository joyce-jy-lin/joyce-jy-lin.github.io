Environmental Epidemiologist

Joyce J.Y. Lin, PhD

I study how environmental and social exposures across the life course shape healthy aging and age-related disease.

My research integrates exposure science, biomonitoring, and causal inference methods to identify sensitive windows, biological pathways, and intervention points for reducing environmental health inequities.

Current position

Postdoctoral Research Fellow, Department of Environmental Health, Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health.

Research themes

Developmental origins of aging

How prenatal, childhood, and early-life environmental exposures influence later-life health and chronic disease.

Environmental and social determinants

How chemical exposures interact with psychosocial resources, stress, and social contexts across the life course.

Biomonitoring and exposure science

Using biomarkers such as baby teeth and toenails to reconstruct environmental exposures across different time windows.
