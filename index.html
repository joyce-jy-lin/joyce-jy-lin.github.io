Environmental Epidemiologist

Joyce Lin, PhD

I study how environmental exposures across the life course shape trajectories of healthy aging and chronic disease.

My research integrates environmental epidemiology, exposure science, biomonitoring, and causal inference methods to understand how experiences during sensitive developmental periods influence health decades later. My work seeks to identify biological and social pathways linking environmental exposures to aging-related outcomes and uncover opportunities for prevention across the lifespan.

Research Areas

Life Course Epidemiology

Investigating sensitive windows of environmental and social exposures that shape health trajectories across the life span.

Biomonitoring and Exposure Assessment

Developing and applying biomarkers to characterize environmental exposures across the life course.

Chemical and Psychosocial Co-Exposures

Understanding how chemical exposures interact with psychosocial and structural factors to influence health outcomes.
