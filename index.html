Environmental Epidemiologist

Joyce J.Y. Lin, PhD

I study how environmental and social exposures across the life course shape healthy aging and age-related disease.

My research integrates environmental epidemiology, exposure science, biomonitoring, and causal inference methods to identify sensitive windows, biological pathways, and intervention points for reducing environmental health inequities.

Research focus

Life-course environmental epidemiology

How exposures during prenatal, childhood, and early-life periods shape health decades later.

Healthy aging and chronic disease

Environmental and social determinants of aging-related outcomes, including mental health, cognitive health, obesity, and cardiometabolic disease.

Biomonitoring and exposure assessment

Novel biomarkers such as toenails and baby teeth to reconstruct environmental exposures across different life stages.
