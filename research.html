Research

My research program investigates how environmental exposures across the life course influence trajectories of aging and chronic disease.

Developmental origins of aging

I study how exposures during sensitive developmental periods shape health decades later, with a focus on early-life environmental toxicants and later-life outcomes.

Environmental exposures and chronic disease

My work examines how metals and environmental toxicants contribute to aging-related outcomes including mental health, cognitive health, obesity, and cardiometabolic disease.

Social and environmental determinants

I am interested in how psychosocial resources, stress, and social environments modify the health impacts of environmental exposures across the life course.

Exposure science and biomonitoring

My methodological work uses biomarkers such as toenails and baby teeth to measure exposures across different life stages and improve environmental epidemiologic studies.
