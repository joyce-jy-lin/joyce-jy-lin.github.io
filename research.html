Research

My research program investigates how environmental exposures across the life course influence trajectories of aging and chronic disease.

Developmental Origins of Disease

I study how exposures during sensitive developmental periods shape health decades later, with a focus on understanding how environmental factors influence aging trajectories and chronic disease risk across the life course.

Environmental Exposures and Brain Health

My work examines how metals and other environmental toxicants contribute to brain-related outcomes, including mental health and cognitive function.

Mechanisms Linking Exposure and Disease

I investigate biological and behavioral pathways, including psychosocial stress, cardiometabolic health, and epigenetic processes.

Environmental and Social Determinants of Aging

I am interested in how psychosocial resources, stress, and social environments modify the health impacts of environmental exposures and contribute to disparities in healthy aging.

Exposure Science and Biomonitoring

My methodological work leverages biomarkers such as toenails and baby teeth to characterize environmental exposures across different life stages and improve environmental epidemiologic research.
